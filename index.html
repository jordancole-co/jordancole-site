The Philosophy

Forget everything you've ever heard about meditation.

For a long time, meditation felt like something that belonged to other people. People with incense and mantras and the ability to sit cross-legged without their legs falling asleep.

It's not that. Not here, anyway.

Here, we look at meditation the same way we'd look at a screwdriver — it's just a tool.

An incredibly simple and powerful tool.

You don't need to believe in anything. You don't need to change who you are. You just need five minutes and the willingness to sit still long enough to hear what you already know.

Whatever you're looking for — less stress, feeling less stuck, a clearer sense of direction — you already know how to find it. You just have to get quiet enough to remember the way.

How It Works

1

Pause. Build an intentional pause into your day. Five minutes of sitting still, observing your breath. That's it.

2

Observe. Your mind will wander. That's not a bug, it's a feature. When you catch a thought, you learn something about yourself.

3

Learn. Over time, the thoughts you keep coming back to become the perfect roadmap to any action you've been overlooking.

4

Take action. Use what you've learned to make real, tangible changes. The sitting and the doing are equally important.

Four Core Principles

The foundation.

01

Within, Not Without

It may feel like you really just want that new Escalade because it's a rad car. But what we're all really after is some form of love, acceptance, peace, or joy. You will never find those things outside of yourself. They can only be found within.

02

Intuition

Meditation is the best tool we have to reconnect to the knowing inside of us. The quiet voice that will guide you back to the love, peace, and joy you've been looking for — when you're still enough to hear it.

03

Reflection

What is going on inside of you is reflected in your external experience. A mind that is more aware of itself can guide you into a more peaceful, loving state. As within, so without.

04

Action

When you feel too afraid to make any moves, that is precisely the time to take action. Stillness and action work together — stilling the mind and breaking through old patterns to bring you closer to your true self.
